Popular Destinations

Tirumala

Tirumala is located 3,200 feet (980 m) above sea level and covers an area of approximately 10.33 square miles (26.8 km2)

Chandragiri Fort

The palace was constructed using stone, brick, lime mortar and devoid of timber. Now it is changed as the museum and place of our old civilazation.

SV Zoo Park

The zoo at the foothills of Tirupati is one of the biggest in India, spread over 5500 acres. It is not an exotic zoo, with animals from across the world but it is a good one.

Talakona Waterfalls

Talakona is home to Talakona Waterfalls, which is one of the highest falls in the country. The word 'talakona' originates from Telugu word 'tala' meaning head and 'kona' meaning hill.

Jain Temple

The idol of Shri Tirupathi Lord Venkatesh Balaji is said by the Jains to be originally of Tirthankara Neminath which the Hindus modified after Shri Aadi Shankaracharya's efforts to revive Hinduism.

Mamandur Forest

Mamandur is bountifully endowed with nature in the form of hills and valleys, many picturesque spots and seasonal waterfalls and ponds of Eastern Ghats.It is located near tirupathi.
